The Nobel Prize – Honoring Excellence Across the World

Every year, the Nobel Prize celebrates those rare individuals whose work has shaped our world—through discoveries, courage, and creativity. Here at Nobel Insight, we explore their journeys and the timeless impact of their contributions.

Let's Explore

How It Started?

The Nobel Prize began in 1901, founded by Alfred Nobel — the inventor of dynamite. His vision was to honor those who brought the greatest benefit to humankind through science, literature, and peace. Over time, it became one of the world’s most respected awards.

Nobel Prize Award Categories

The Nobel Prizes are presented in six distinguished fields — Physics, Chemistry, Physiology or Medicine, Literature, Peace, and Economic Sciences. Each category represents Alfred Nobel’s vision to celebrate the most outstanding contributions that benefit humanity and inspire future generations.

Nobel Prize Awards & Achievements Timeline

1901

The first Nobel Prizes were awarded in Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, and Peace.

1969

The Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences was established, expanding Alfred Nobel’s vision.

2001

The 100th anniversary of the Nobel Prizes was celebrated worldwide with special exhibitions.

2020

Scientific breakthroughs in genetics and climate change received Nobel recognition.
