Nobel Prize FAQ

1. What is the Nobel Prize?

The Nobel Prize is an international award given to individuals or organizations who have made outstanding contributions in Physics, Chemistry, Medicine, Literature, Peace, and Economic Sciences.

2. What is the history of the Nobel Prize?

The Nobel Prize was established in 1895 by Alfred Nobel, a Swedish inventor, engineer, and industrialist, famous for inventing dynamite.

3. Who can be nominated for the Nobel Prize?

Eligibility criteria vary by category. Generally, individuals or organizations who have made extraordinary contributions can be nominated.

4. How are Nobel Prize winners selected?

Winners are selected by committees of experts and past laureates. The final decision is made by the Nobel Committee.

5. How much is the Nobel Prize worth?

The cash award varies each year. In 2024, the prize amount was approximately 11 million Swedish kronor per category.

6. When are the Nobel Prizes announced?

The winners are announced in the first week of October every year. The award ceremony is held on December 10 in Stockholm or Oslo (Peace Prize in Oslo).

7. Why is the Nobel Prize significant?

The Nobel Prize is considered one of the highest recognitions worldwide. It honors scientific, literary, and humanitarian achievements and raises global awareness.
